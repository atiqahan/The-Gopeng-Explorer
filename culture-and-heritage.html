Culture & Heritage

People

The population of Gopeng consists of:

  • Semai
  • Cina Kantonis
  • Cina Hakka
  • Rawa (Urang Rao)
  • Mandailing
  • Melayu Perak
  • Melayu Deli
  • Tamil
  • Punjabi

The town has a mix of Malay, Chinese (especially Hakka), Indian, and Orang Asli (Semai tribe).

People still practice traditional customs and live peacefully together.

The population of Gopeng is estimated to be approximately 100,000 people.

Traditional Food

Kelamai

Kelamai is a chewy, smoky treat made by slow-cooking glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and palm sugar in thick bamboo. A Rawa delicacy, it symbolizes patience, heritage, and festive craftsmanship.

Lei Cha

Lei Cha, or Thunder Tea Rice, is a nourishing Hakka dish made with ground herbs and tea poured over rice, topped with veggies, tofu, pickles, and crunchy bits. It celebrates wellness, balance, and tradition.

Penjan Ubi Kayu

Penjan ubi kayu is a traditional Perak dessert made by simmering boiled cassava in a thick syrup of palm sugar, pandan leaves, and cornflour. Soft, sweet, and fragrant, it’s often enjoyed warm with a cup of kopi kampung—especially in rural homes during late afternoons.

Hakka Noodles

Hakka noodles are stir-fried wheat noodles tossed with crisp vegetables, garlic, and savory sauces like soy and vinegar. Popular in Indo-Chinese cuisine, they’re known for their springy texture and bold, smoky flavor. Perfect as a quick street-style meal or festive comfort food.
